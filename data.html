Corpus Annotation

The NLPContributionTask comprises a corpus of scholarly articles in Natural Language Processing research focused on Machine Learning applications and was annotated as follows:

  1. The annotator identified contribution-related sentences, entities, and their triples
  2. In an adjudication phase, the annotations were revised as seen necessary

The annotation methodology can be found described in the arxiv article here.

Data Format

The NLPContribution data is in the following format. For the training and dev sets, for each annotated scholarly article in the corpus, there will be a directory. That directory will contain a plain text file which is the article's full text, preprocessed for tokenization and sentence splitting; and there will be three text files: Sentences.txt will contain the annotated contribution sentence numbers from the full-text file; Entity.txt will contain the sentence number, tab-separated from the start and end token numbers of the annotated entity in the sentence; and Triples.txt will contain the triples of the entity annotations, unless the predicates are inferred additionally, in which case they will be shown as strings. Samples of the annotations are released as trial data.
